What is ESL and Why it is Important

ESL stands for English as a Second Language. These programs teach English to people whose first language is not English.

Helps people communicate in daily life, get better jobs, access education and healthcare, and become more independent.

Without English skills, many people face barriers that limit their opportunities.

ESL programs have several important benefits:

Economic benefits

  • Helps people qualify for better jobs
  • Strengthens the workforce

Social benefits

  • Builds stronger communities
  • Encourages participation in society

Personal benefits

  • Increases confidence
  • Improves quality of life
